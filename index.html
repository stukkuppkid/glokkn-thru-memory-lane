Biography

Alexander Gabriel Quezada, known professionally as Glokk40Spaz, was born on August 29, 2003, in Decatur, Georgia. He began his musical journey in 2019, drawing early inspiration from artists such as D. Savage and the underground SoundCloud movement that emphasized raw authenticity over polish. His stage name reflects both his energetic, unpredictable delivery style — often described as “spazzing out” — and his recurring lyrical imagery surrounding Glock firearms, which became a symbolic part of his branding. From the beginning, his music blended gangsta rap themes with trap production and dark plugg aesthetics, forming a sound that felt aggressive, emotional, and unfiltered.

Starting with independent SoundCloud releases, he quickly built traction in the underground rap community. His affiliation with EBG Records connected him with collaborators such as BabySolid and 2wo2imes, strengthening his local presence in the Decatur and Atlanta scenes. In August 2022, he was arrested on armed robbery charges, a moment that significantly altered his career trajectory. Despite incarceration, his popularity grew as previously recorded material was released strategically. Briefly signing with Columbia Records before returning to independence, Glokk40Spaz emerged from jail after roughly two years, continuing his career while navigating probation. His music consistently centers on street survival, resilience, loyalty, confrontation, and the psychological impact of his environment, maintaining a distinct underground identity.

Era 1: Early Beginnings (2019–2020)

Glokk40Spaz’s early career from 2019 to 2020 marked the foundational phase of his development in the underground rap scene. Although he began experimenting with music in late 2018, 2019 was the year his name started circulating. On June 16, 2019, he released his debut project “Don’t Get Took Off” under EBG Records. This album introduced his raw, unpolished sound — heavily influenced by street life in Decatur, Georgia. The production leaned toward gritty trap beats layered with minimal but haunting melodies, creating space for his intense vocal tone. Lyrically, he focused on survival, loyalty, confrontation, and navigating conflict. References to firearms and neighborhood affiliations were frequent, reflecting authenticity rather than mainstream marketability.

In December 2020, he followed with “Don’t Get Took Off 2,” which refined his delivery and structure. The beats became harder, and his vocal presence more confident. While still aggressive, there were moments of introspection regarding hustling, paranoia, and avoiding legal trouble. This era emphasized solo presence over heavy collaboration, though he frequently referenced EBG affiliates like Go.unna and Lil EBG. His style evolved from freestyle-heavy recordings to more structured song formats, incorporating subtle auto-tune for emotional layering without abandoning rawness. By the end of 2020, he had built a dedicated underground audience drawn to his authenticity and intensity, laying the groundwork for future expansion.

Era 2: Rising Underground (2021–2022)

The 2021–2022 period represented Glokk40Spaz’s ascent within the underground rap community. In July 2021, he released “2wo Glokks” alongside 2wo2imes, highlighting chemistry between collaborators and solidifying his presence within the Atlanta underground. The project showcased aggressive flows over darker trap instrumentals. Throughout 2021, his confidence and pacing improved, and his name circulated more widely through social media and repost culture.

In 2022, his output became prolific. “Gone M.A.D” with BabySolid explored darker plugg sounds, while April’s “BABY WHOA” marked a breakthrough moment. With twenty tracks, the mixtape demonstrated versatility, blending melodic hooks with intense verses. Songs like “Be Right Back” and “Remember Me” increased his visibility significantly. He followed this momentum with “Spaz&B” and “Don’t Get Took Off 3,” reinforcing continuity within his catalog. Themes of loyalty, betrayal, confrontation, and street survival dominated this era. His arrest in August 2022 on armed robbery charges abruptly shifted the narrative of his career. Despite legal challenges, the material recorded during house arrest and prior sessions ensured his voice remained present, and his buzz only intensified following incarceration.

Era 3: Incarceration Breakthrough (2023–2024)

Between 2023 and 2024, Glokk40Spaz experienced an unusual form of career acceleration while incarcerated in Fulton County Jail. With a vault of pre-recorded material, his team strategically released projects that maintained momentum. In June 2023, “Glokk Files” compiled notable earlier tracks while introducing new material, functioning as both reintroduction and consolidation of his growing fanbase.

September 2023 brought “Took The Biggest Risk,” his debut studio album, which carried symbolic weight considering his legal situation. The album featured seventeen tracks and highlighted a darker, more reflective tone. In March 2024, the deluxe edition “After TTBR” expanded the narrative. Later in 2024, “Da Real OSO” and the EP “Slaughter House Recordz” further solidified his productivity during incarceration. Themes in this era leaned heavily into reflection, defiance, paranoia, and resilience. The incarceration period paradoxically amplified his mystique, as fans gravitated toward the authenticity and tension embedded within the music. Upon release around late 2024, he re-entered the public space with heightened recognition and a strengthened underground presence.

Era 4: Post-Release & Independence (2025–2026)

Following his release, Glokk40Spaz entered a new independent chapter marked by maturity and creative expansion. In August 2025, he released “Baby Whoa 2,” serving as a sequel to his earlier breakthrough mixtape. The project blended pluggnb melodies with refined trap production, reflecting both growth and continuity. A deluxe edition in January 2026 expanded the tracklist significantly, demonstrating productivity and independence.

This era emphasized artistic control, strategic collaborations, and polished production quality. While themes of street credibility and resilience remained, there was increased reflection on incarceration, loyalty shifts, and navigating success. Touring appearances and digital engagement strengthened his reach beyond the underground niche. Rather than relying on controversy, this period focused on controlled evolution. By 2026, he stood as an independent artist who had survived legal setbacks, industry transitions, and shifting trends while maintaining a distinctive identity rooted in Atlanta’s underground rap culture.
